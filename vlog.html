My Top 3 Vlogs


Sik-ka, means pulling out the rice seedlings from where it was sown for rice planting. Rice planting season in our country starts at May or after several consistent rains at the start of the rainy seasons. The first step is sowing then Sik-ka is done after a month when the seedlings reach up to 8-12 inches. This is done a day before transplanting which is the next vlog below.

Click here to watch Si-ka in Youtube



Ra-ep, is our dialect's term for transplanting rice. It is done, usually in the months of June to August when rain is plenty in the Philippines. In this, vlog you can see the steps on how it is traditionally done, since we are still using the standard machineries in rice planting. From waking up early in the morning to transport the seedlings to the farm, to iriigating the soil, planting each seedling manually using the hands of our farmers until sunset.
